1. Synopsis

This project involves exploring storm database maintained by the U.S. National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration. This database maintains records of various weather events in US and their effects on society.

The purpose of this report is to answer two main questions as follows

  • Across the United States, which types of events (as indicated in the EVTYPE variable) are most harmful with respect to population health?

  • Across the United States, which types of events have the greatest economic consequences?

Data cleaning and processing

Following chunk will subset the data. And change the letter case of EVTYPE column. Changing letter case is important as without that the program assumes events like Coastal Flooding, COASTAL FLOODING and coastal flooding as separate entities. This skews results.

#SUBSETTING THE DATA
Subset_Storm_Data <- Storm_Data[, c("EVTYPE", "FATALITIES", "INJURIES", "PROPDMG", "PROPDMGEXP", "CROPDMG", "CROPDMGEXP")]

#Change the case
## change class to character so the below loop works
Subset_Storm_Data$EVTYPE <- as.character(Subset_Storm_Data$EVTYPE) 
#loop
Subset_Storm_Data <- data.frame(lapply(Subset_Storm_Data, function(v) {
    if (is.character(v)) return(toupper(v))
    else return(v)
}))

## Apply ddply on fatalities and injuries.
library(plyr)
Health_Storm_Data <- ddply(Subset_Storm_Data, "EVTYPE", summarize, Totfat = sum(FATALITIES), Totinj = sum(INJURIES))

As you can see from the graphs, Tornado is the deadliest weather event. The next most fatal event, which is excessive heat, is not even at 50% as compared to Tornados. With that said though, always stay hydrated and wear a hat to avoid heat stroke. The other candidates in the top six killers are Heat, Flash Flooding, Lightning and Thunderstorms. These events in the same order cause injuries too.

For the monetary damage, the first thing to notice is that magnitude of property damage is way to high as compared to the magnitude of crop damage. This is consistent as generally an acre of farmland produces 1000 dollars in a season on the other hand even a little damage to a car or a roof of the house will cause much higher repair bills.

Also from the graph it is clear that the worst events for the property damage is Flood followed by Hurricane and Tornado. Hail and Flashflood made the last two of the top six and I think thats because of the damage they cause to automobiles in the urban area.

For Crops, obviously draught is the most devastating. Followed by Flood and Hurricane. River flood also seems to be logical as lot of farmland is in the vicinity of rivers. Ice storm and Hail destroy plant life especially when they are out of the season and they form the last two of the top six crop damaging weather events.

The last plot is essentially the sum of property damage and crop damage by a particular weather event. With so much of difference between property and crop damage aggregate values, we wont really see any different Weather Events in this graph than the ones that we see in Property Damage graph. Still this gives us a total picture of economic damage caused by the top 10 types of weather event.
